Some Hikes

A brief assortment of some hikes I have enjoyed in no particular order

Hood

Beautiful winter hike, crunching snow and a frigid chill. Incredible view of Mount Hood through the trees

Olympic Lake

After failing to drive up the road to Olympic National Park (apparently it's dangerous to drive on frozen roads), we were able to find a wonderful hike up to a lake.

The Gorge

Carved by the columbia river, The Gorge is one of the most beatiful areas I've come across in my travels. Endless waterfalls, hikes, and magnificent mountains are just some of the features of this beautiful area

Bridges

A handcrafted bridge up to the lake pictured above. Incredibly fun to walk across

River

Just 20 minutes from our apartment. This picture does little justice to depict the majesty of this river. Easy to access and a stunning site

Flowers

The desctructive force of Mount St Helens left a desolate, almost moon like landscape in its wake. Years later, flowers and strawberries bloom in the rich soils nourished by volcanic ash

High Desert Falls

In eastern Washington you will find a vast landscape, sparsley vegated except for agrigcultural crops and shrubs. Pullman falls is a must see if you are in the area

Helen Rivers

The most prestine shades of blue I have ever seen. As you walk closer and closer to the Helen the waters surrounding the area falls in your sight

Silver Star Mountain

A tough hike, but the view from the top is well worth the work. Hood, Helens, Adams, and more that I cannot name at this point surround you from all sides at Silver Star Mountain's peak.
